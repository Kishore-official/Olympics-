Football

game in which two teams of 11 players, using any part of their bodies except their hands and arms, try to maneuver the ball into the opposing team's goal

Cricket

Cricket is played with a bat and ball and involves two competing sides (teams) of 11 players. The field is oval with a rectangular area in the middle, known as the pitch, that is 22 yards (20.12 metres) by 10 feet (3.04 metres) wide

Badminton

The game involves the two opponents hitting the shuttlecock over the net into the other person's side. The rally ends when the shuttlecock touches the ground. Only one stroke is allowed to pass it over the net

Golf

a cross-country game in which a player strikes a small ball with various clubs from a series of starting points (teeing grounds) into a series of holes on a course

Basketball

a cross-country game in which a player strikes a small ball with various clubs from a series of starting points (teeing grounds) into a series of holes on a course

Athletic100m

the standard distance over which the 'world's fastest man or women' is determined and the shortest distance contested on the Olympic track and field programme

swimming

The Olympic sized pool is 165 feet long by 56 feet wide (8 lap lanes, each 7 feet wide). It holds 490,000 gallons (almost 1/2 million gallons).

Rugby

In Olympic rugby, three forwards of each team face each other in a single line, while the other form of rugby usually has eight players from each side pushing against each other rugby are kicked as drop goals and not from a tee

Tennis

Table Tennis became an Olympic sport in Seoul 1988, the play is fast and demands quick reactions. The aim of the game is to hit the light ball back and forth over the table with small bats, the winner of the game is the first to 11 points

Boxing

A boxer wins a bout at the Olympics by accumulating the most points over three rounds a winner is decided by the judging criteria and is awarded 10 points. The round's loser receives somewhere between 7 to 9 points based on how they performed
